You're a size queen huh ? Here are some large plants

1. Fiddle leaf fig

Bright, indirect light is ideal. Only water this plant when the top inch of the soil is dry. Place it in a corner to create a dramatic focal point, which we know you'll enjoy you drama queen.

2. Bird of paradise

Let this baby grow in a bright, sunny spot and it will definitely prosper, but it can handle lower light levels, too. You'll need to fertilize it often in the spring and summer, but otherwise it's fairly simple to handle—so long as you don't overwater it.

3. Madagascar dragon tree

Spiky leaves that grow upwards and have a red outline make the name of this plant absolutely perfect. But direct sunlight could damage them—so give this guy a mix of sun and shade. Be cautious, toxic to both dogs and cats, keep animals far away.

4. Bamboo palm

Bamboo palms are pretty low-maintenance and they do surprisingly well in a variety of lighting conditions. They do require consistent moisture, so if you tend to under-water your plants you may want to skip this palm.

5. Heart-leaf philodendron

Harder to kill than to keep alive, this beauty will do just fine no matter what you throw at her. She's a fighter Roberta, so just make sure to not forget her water to keep direct sunlight away.

6. Monstera

Add this little guy to a shelf and watch it trail down. Make sure that you place it in bright, indirect sunlight and water every one to two weeks.
