Small flat eh ? Enjoy these itty bitty small plants

1. Marble queen pothos

This marvelous queen prefers her soil to be on the dry side. Water when the top several inches of soil has dried out. When watering, water the plant with fresh water lightly and slowly from above, then tip out any excess water. Water moderately and infrequently.

2. Anthurium

Known for its waxy, heart-shaped flowers with a prominent stamen in the center, this plant needs to be watered every one to two weeks and requires occasional fertilizing—so they're not low-maintenance, but not high-maintenance, either. Just like you.

3. Aglaonema

Keep your aglaonema plant's soil just barely moist—not wet—and avoid allowing the soil to dry out completely. Generally, it will need watering more frequently in the spring and summer and less in the winterYour plant will tell you when it's getting too dry by letting its leaves droop.

4. Chinese evergreen

Chinese evergreen plants thrive in medium to low light conditions or indirect sunlight. Wherever you place, just make sure that she receives warm temps and somewhat humid conditions. However, this flexible babe will tolerate less than ideal conditions if necessary.

5. Rattlesnake plant

This gorgeous tropical plant thrives in moist, warm, semi-shady climates (this your Tinder bio ?). It will need lots of heat and humidity to keep this plant happy and healthy, but you'll be rewarded with bright green leaves.

6. String of pearl

Add this little guy to a shelf and watch it trail down. Make sure that you place it in bright, indirect sunlight and water every one to two weeks.

7. Red prayer plant

Place your Red Prayer Plant in bright, indirect light. Avoid putting it in direct sun—too much sun will fade the leaf colors and burn the foliage. Water your Prayer Plant when the top 25% of the soil is dry.
